ABSTRACT  
The implementation of an optimal power scheduling strategy is vital for the optimal design of the integrated 
electric vehicle (EV) charging station with photovoltaic (PV) and battery energy storage system (BESS). However, 
traditional design methods always neglect accurate PV power modeling and adopt overly simplistic EV charging 
strategies, which might result in suboptimal and infeasible designs. This study proposes a novel simultaneous 
capacity configuration and scheduling optimization model for PV/BESS integrated EV charging stations, which 
combines hybrid modeling for PV power prediction and optimal scheduling method for charging piles. The 
original model is then converted to a mixed integer linear programming problem by the Big-M method to greatly 
reduce the computational complexity. A typical scenario of commercial region is employed to demonstrate the 
effectiveness of the proposed approach. The results indicate that the approach could improve economic benefits 
by 15.67 % and reduce carbon emissions by 37.14 % compared with the entire grid-based mode. Besides, 
integrating the PV hybrid modeling method and optimal charging scheduling strategy could achieve superior 
capacity configurations and greatly improve the comprehensive performance of the system. It is thus illustrated 
that the approach could provide a promising solution for the optimal design and scheduling of PV/BESS inte -
grated EV charging stations.   
1. Introduction 
The integrated electric vehicle charging station (EVCS) with photo -
voltaic (PV) and battery energy storage system (BESS) has attracted 
increasing attention [ 1 ]. This integrated charging station could be 
greatly helpful for reducing the EV ’ s electricity demand for the main 
grid [ 2 ], restraining the fluctuation and uncertainty of PV power gen -
eration [ 3 ], and consequently improving the renewable energy utiliza -
tion ratio [ 4 ]. Although a large number of demonstration projects of the 
integrated charging station have been constructed and their technical 
feasibility has been validated, the capacity configuration of both PV and 
BESS are usually determined by empirical methods [ 5 ]. In general, it has 
been recognized that the implementation of optimal power scheduling 
strategy with accurate historical PV power generation and EV charging 
load information could play a significant role in assisting in the optimal 
design of the integrated charging station [ 6 ]. Hence, the simultaneous 
capacity configuration and scheduling optimization approach is of vital 
importance to enhance the comprehensive performance of the 
integrated charging station [ 7 ]. 
Many approaches have been developed for optimizing the capacities 
of key equipment and enhancing the performances of the PV/BESS in -
tegrated charging stations in previous researches. Economic perfor -
mance indicators are the most commonly used objectives in the 
optimization process, such as net present value (NPV) [ 8 ], levelized cost 
of electricity [ 9 ], return on investment [ 10 ], equipment install cost [ 11 ], 
etc. Some studies also simultaneously optimize other performance in -
dicators in terms of technical and environmental, like energy 
self-consumption ratio [ 7 ], power loss [ 12 ], carbon emission [ 4 ], EV 
served ratio [ 13 ], etc. Rodriguez et al. [ 14 ] analyze the technical per -
formance, CO
2 
emission reduction, and financial attractiveness of PV-EV 
systems based on typical scenarios from four countries. Bartolucci et al. 
[ 4 ] propose a multi-objective optimization model for a PV/BESS assisted 
EV charging station, which incorporates two objectives of minimizing 
carbon emissions and maximizing the self-consumption of local energy 
production. Compared to the extensive application of various objectives 
and optimization algorithms in the capacity configuration phase, the

PV output power profiles play a significant role in determining the 
charging/discharging strategies of BESS and the trading behaviors be -
tween charging stations and the main grid. However, accurate predic -
tion of PV power remains a challenge due to the inherent stochastic, 
intermittent, and fluctuating characteristics of solar energy [ 16 ]. In 
previous researches on the capacity configuration of PV/BESS integrated 
charging stations, PV output power is always described as a linear 
function of solar irradiance and ambient temperature [ 17 ]. Neverthe -
less, previous studies indicate that PV output power exhibits a complex 
nonlinear relationship with both meteorological conditions and internal 
parameters, which is always described by nonlinear models such as the 
equivalent circuit model (ECM) and artificial neural network (ANN) 
[ 18 ]. Therefore, employing a simple linear model might result in inac -
curate PV output power profiles, rendering the capacity configuration 
and scheduling based on them suboptimal or infeasible. Hence, more 
accurate PV modeling methods should be utilized to obtain optimal. 
Furthermore, the determination of the scheduling strategy for 
charging piles is a crucial aspect of scheduling optimization, and also 
plays a significant role in determining the optimal capacity configura -
tion of integrated EV stations. Previous studies always assume the 
charging demand of EVs as a constant power profile [ 9 ], or employ 
simplistic rules to assign the power of charging piles, such as assuming 
that EVs would be charged at maximum power upon arrival at the 
charging piles [ 19 ]. However, this overly simplistic charging strategy 
neglects the impact of factors such as the current power generation 
ability of PV/BESS and time-of-use electricity prices of the integrated 
charging station [ 20 ]. This might lead to excessive peak charging load 
and prolonged idle time after charging [ 21 ], thereby reducing the eco -
nomic benefits of the charging station [ 22 ]. Integrating the charging 
scheduling model and constraints into the scheduling optimization 
process and conducting a comprehensive economic evaluation of the 
charging station, could achieve the optimal scheduling strategy of 
charging piles [ 23 ]. This strategy could effectively reduce the 
peak-to-valley difference of charging load [ 24 ], as well as improve the 
consumption ratio of PV power generation [ 25 ]. Despite the promising 
potential of such optimal charging strategies, previous studies have 
primarily focused on the impact of such strategies on the scheduling 
process [ 26 ], and the influence of such strategies on the capacity 
configuration of PV/BESS integrated EV charging stations still lacks 
systematic analysis. 
Table 1 shows a brief evaluation of the capacity configuration and 
scheduling optimization methods for PV/BESS integrated EV charging 
stations or similar systems published in previous literature. Within the 
context of previous research, there are two salient contributions deliv -
ered to this paper: (1) a hybrid modeling approach for PV power pre -
diction and an optimal scheduling method for charging piles are 
developed to accurately describe the source/load power characteristics 
d for the PV/BESS integrated charging station; (2) a simultaneous ca -
pacity configuration and scheduling optimization approach is proposed 
to enhance both the economic and environmental performances in the 
whole lifecycle of the system. Moreover, to reduce the computational 
cost of the optimization process, the Big-M linearization method is 
employed to reformulate this model into a mixed integer linear pro -
gramming (MILP) problem. 
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Firstly, the 
typical configuration of the PV/BESS integrated EV charging station and 
the proposed simultaneous capacity configuration and scheduling opti -
mization model are introduced. Subsequently, the optimization results 
for a typical scenario in China are presented, and the influence of 
different PV models and charging pile scheduling strategies on optimal 
capacity configuration is analyzed. Then a sensitivity analysis of key 
parameters is implemented and some meaningful conclusions are 
concluded. Finally, the conclusion is drawn.

consists of PV, BESS, EV charging piles, transformer (T), and main grid.

energy could then be utilized to charge EVs during periods of electricity 
shortages. Besides, the electricity interaction between the main grid and 
the DC bus bar is also bi-directional through a transformer. This allows 
the integrated charging station to sell electricity back to the main grid 
when there is a surplus of energy and to purchase electricity when there

Based on the above typical topology of the PV/BESS integrated EV 
charging station, the simultaneous capacity configuration and sched -
uling optimization problem is formulated to determine the optimal ca -
pacity configurations and operating strategies of different equipment. In 
this optimization problem, the preferred strategies should satisfy the 
electricity demands for EVs within specified time periods, aiming to 
maximize both the economic and environmental benefits of the system. 
Hence, the optimization problem could be represented as follows: 
Input: (1) the hourly profiles of weather conditions, including solar 
irradiance, ambient temperature, and wind speed; (2) the parameters of 
EV behaviors, including EV arrival time interval, charging demand, and 
extra residence time; (3) time-of-use electricity price; (4) economic, 
environmental, and technical parameters of PV, BESS, and transformer. 
Output: (1) optimal capacities of PV, BESS, and transformer; (2) 
typical annual scheduling strategy of the integrated charging station; (3) 
economic, environmental, and technical performance evaluation 
indicators. 
Objective: maximize the comprehensive NPV during both the in -
vestment and operation process. 
System models: (1) hybrid model for PV system; (2) stochastic 
simulation model for EV charging behaviors. 
Main constraints: (1) design constraints; (2) energy balance con -
straints; (3) constraints for optimal scheduling of charging piles; (4) 
scheduling constraints of BESS and transformer. 
Furthermore, the main variables and parameters used in the input, 
output, and objective of the optimization problem are listed in Table 2 . 
The specific functions of the above variables and other intermediate 
variables in the optimization problem will be described in Section 3 . 
The implementation procedure of the optimization approach is 
shown in Fig. 2 . In this approach, the PV generation power and EV 
charging demand characteristics are firstly derived by proposed system 
models, utilizing input parameters such as weather conditions and pa -
rameters of EV behaviors. Then the obtained characteristics are utilized 
as inputs for the optimization model, where computations are performed 
to determine the optimal capacities for crucial equipment and formulate 
efficient scheduling strategies.

3.2.3. Other evaluation indicators 
Although the objective function NPV utilized in this optimization 
model provides a comprehensive economic indicator for evaluating 
system performance, additional evaluation indicators are introduced to 
achieve a multi-dimensional evaluation of optimization results. The 
descriptions and mathematical equations of evaluation indicators are 
shown in Table 3 . 
Based on Eqs. ((10) – (24) and (26) – (29) and (32) – (34) and (36) , an 
MILP model is developed for the simultaneous optimal capacity 
configuration and scheduling of PV/BESS integrated EV charging station 
system. 
The proposed optimization model is solved by the Gurobi optimizer 
via GAMS, with a relative optimization gap of 0.001 % and a maximum 
run time of 5000 s. The computation platform is an Intel(R) Xeon(R) 
CPU Gold 5218 @2.30 GHz computer with 16.00 GB RAM. Utilizing the 
branch and bound method, the Gurobi optimizer always could find both 
feasible and proven optimal solutions with a superior speed compared to 
other solvers [ 38 ].

4. Data description 
A typical scenario of an EV charging station in the Shanghai com -
mercial region, China, is developed to validate the effectiveness of the 
proposed simultaneous optimization model. In this scenario, the 
generated electricity of the system is only unitized by itself, and the 
residual energy is prohibited to connect to the main grid. The system 
lifecycle is set as 20 years and the time interval of the scheduling process 
is 1 h. The discount rate is fixed at 6 % and the unit of all economic data 
is Chinese Yuan (CNY). 
One-year experimental data of a PV rooftop commercial plant is 
collected for the validation of the proposed PV hybrid modeling method. 
The collected data consists of solar irradiance, ambient temperature, 
wind speed, PV cell temperature, output power, etc. The total annual 
solar irradiance is about 1385.93 kWh ⋅ m
  2
. The measured average PV 
output power for each month is shown in Fig. S-1 of the Supplementary 
Material. 
The parameters of charging piles and EVs are shown in Table 4 , and 
the expected time interval distribution for EV arrivals is described in 
Fig. 5 . It can be seen that the peak of EV charging demand appears be -
tween 8:00 – 10:00. Utilizing the proposed stochastic simulation method 
of EV behaviors, the integrated charging station would accommodate 
approximately 29604 EVs each year, and the total annual electricity 
demand is about 755.20 MWh.

5. Results and discussion 
In this section, the optimization results based on the typical scenario 
are analyzed including both design capacities and scheduling strategies. 
Then the impact of different PV models and scheduling methods of 
charging piles on the optimization results are compared. Finally, sensi -
tivity analysis of key parameters is implemented to analyze the impact of 
key parameters on the optimization results. 
5.1. Optimization result analysis 
The proposed simultaneous optimal capacity configuration and 
scheduling optimization model is solved based on the above scenario. 
The capacity configuration optimization results and evaluation in -
dicators are shown in Table 6 . It can be observed that the optimal ca -
pacities of PV, BESS, and transformer are 221.61 kW, 79.96 kWh, and 
114.70 kVA, respectively. The NPV of the PV/BESS integrated EV 
charging station system for the whole lifecycle is 9.86 Mil. CNY. 
Compared to the entire grid-based EV charging station, the proposed 
method could reduce the whole lifecycle carbon emission by 37.14 % 
and increase the economic benefit by 15.67 %. Besides, the result shows 
high energy efficiency of 97.55 %, while the abandonment rate of PV 
generation energy is only 4.88 %. the daily mean number of BESS 
charging/discharging cycle is 0.85, which is a trade-off between battery 
life and cost. 
According to the scheduling optimization results, the annual energy 
flow chart of the PV/BESS integrated EV charging station system is 
shown in Fig. 6 . It can be seen that the annual electricity of PV gener -
ation and purchased from the main grid are 301.65 and 472.49 MWh, 
which accounts for about 38.97 % and 61.03 % of the whole energy 
source of the system, respectively. About 94.93 % of input electricity 
from the bus bar is directly utilized for charging EVs, while 3.17 % is 
stored in the BESS. Besides, about 14.71 MWh of PV generation energy 
cannot be utilized, accounting for 77.70 % of the system energy loss. The 
remaining 22.29 % energy loss is attributed to the BESS charging/dis -
charging process. 
To capture the output power characteristics of different equipment at 
different time periods in a day, the diurnal mean power profiles of 
typical power flows from 0:00 to 24:00 are calculated and shown in 
Fig. 7 , including PV generation power, charging/discharging power of 
BESS, purchased power from the main grid, abandoned power, and the 
power demand of EVs. The diurnal mean power represents the mean 
power of specific time periods of the whole year. From Fig. 7 , it can be 
observed that the daily mean power demand of EVs is almost higher than 
the PV generation power in different time periods, while the power and 
energy gap is covered by the electricity from the main grid, especially 
from 15:00 to 6:00. Note that the profile of the purchased power shows 
three valleys at time periods at 9:00 – 11:00, 14:00 – 15:00 and 
19:00 – 21:00. This might because that the electricity prices at the above 
time periods are peak prices and the cost of purchasing electricity from 
main grid is expensive. The occurrence of abandoned electricity is 
mainly observed between 9:00 – 15:00, possibly due to the BESS being 
fully charged, and the excessive generation electricity from the PV sys -
tem cannot be stored.

5.2. Comparison of optimization results with different PV models 
The selection of PV models plays an important role in the PV power 
prediction accuracy, and further affects the optimization capacity 
configuration results of the PV/BESS integrated EV charging station. In 
this study, based on the experimental data of a PV rooftop commercial 
plant shown in Section 4 , the PV power prediction performances of the 
traditional linear model and the proposed hybrid modeling method are 
first evaluated. The ten-fold cross-validation approach is introduced to 
test the fitting and prediction accuracy of the proposed hybrid modeling 
method. Then, we utilize the output power prediction results of two PV 
models to implement the simultaneous optimization model and compare 
the optimization capacity configuration results. 
For a single 310 W PV module, the root mean squared errors (RMSEs) 
during 10 sets of training and testing within the ten-fold cross-validation 
for different PV models are shown in Table 7 . The prediction error dis -
tributions and probability distribution curves for different PV models are 
shown in Fig. 9 . It can be seen that the fitting and prediction RMSEs in 
the simple linear model are 10.75 and 10.56 W, respectively. The fitting 
and prediction RMSEs in the proposed hybrid model are 6.88 and 7.22 
W, respectively. The output power prediction RMSE in the proposed 
hybrid model is 31.63 % lower than that of the simple linear model. This 
highlights the excellent performance of the proposed hybrid model in 
predicting PV output power on the one-year experimental data of a PV 
rooftop commercial plant. 
Furthermore, the optimal capacities of PV, BESS, and transformer 
based on output power prediction results of two PV models are 
compared in Fig. 10 . Note that the optimal capacities based on the 
measured data of PV power are obtained as the theoretical standard 
design. The relative errors between the equipment capacities optimized 
based on PV models and the standard design, are employed to represent 
the deviation from the optimal solutions. From Fig. 10 , it can be 
observed that the optimal capacities of PV, BESS, and transformer in the 
standard design are 223.84 kW, 79.61 kWh, and 113.96 kVA, respec -
tively. Based on the proposed PV hybrid model, the optimal capacities of 
PV, BESS, and transformer are all close to those in the standard design, 
with relative errors of only   0.99 %, 0.44 %, and 0.64 %, respectively. 
Besides, based on the simple linear PV model, the optimal capacities of 
PV, BESS, and transformer are 213.32 kW, 82.44 kWh, and 115.84 kVA, 
with relative errors of   4.70 %, 3.55 %, and 1.64 %, respectively. The 
design is obviously deviated from the standard design, which might 
result in the infeasibility of the scheduling process. Hence, the simul -
taneous optimization model based on the proposed hybrid modeling 
method of PV power prediction could achieve a superior design of PV/ 
BESS integrated EV charging stations. It is worth noting that the effec -
tiveness of our approach, which is a novelty in comparison to existing 
papers, can be expected based on these favorable results.

optimal scheduling strategy. Thus, the simultaneous optimization model 
with an optimal scheduling strategy of charging piles could significantly 
reduce the design capacity of BESS and achieve higher economic ben -
efits, compared to the traditional maximum power strategy.

5.4.1. Sensitivity analysis of the solar irradiance 
The stochastic and intermittent characteristics of solar irradiance 
could significantly affect the scheduling strategies and design capacities 
of the PV/BESS integrated EV charging station. Besides, the difference of 
regional solar irradiance conditions is also the main factor affecting the 
generalizability of the design. Hence, the optimization results with 
different solar irradiances are compared and shown in Fig. 13 (a) and (b). 
The x-axis represents the ratios of the hourly solar irradiance to the 
original values in the basic scenario shown in Section 4 . 
From Fig. 13 , with the solar irradiance increasing, the PV capacity 
decreases from 264.35 to 199.39 kW, while the BESS capacity increases 
from 56.67 to 96.04 kWh. This might be because stronger PV irradiance 
brings higher generation power per kW capacity, but the electricity 
demand of charging piles is still constant, which results in the decrease 
of the PV capacity. Besides, a larger capacity of the BESS would be 
designed to store more excess PV generation electricity during the 
daytime to cover the energy demand at night. As shown in Fig. 13 (b), the 
NPV would increase with the solar irradiance increasing due to the 
lower cost of electricity generation. However, the abandonment rate of 
PV generation electricity still increases from 1.34 % to 10.47 % as the 
ratio of the solar irradiance increases from 70 % to 130 %, despite 
employing a higher capacity of the BESS. Hence, the issue of electricity 
wastage in the PV/BESS integrated EV charging station should be given 
greater attention, particularly in regions with high solar irradiance. To 
address this issue, it is crucial to implement stringent energy efficiency 
constraints and consider higher capacities for BESS.

5.5. Scope and further application of the optimization model 
The proposed optimization model could serve as a fundamental tool 
to support the capacity configuration and scheduling strategy formula -
tion for the PV/BESS integrated EV charging station. Its application has 
the potential to significantly enhance the economic and environmental 
sustainability of such charging stations, thereby promoting their wide -
spread application across different regions. Besides, the proposed opti -
mization model is universal enough to be applied to different types of 
grid connections, including connected and islanded systems, by adjust -
ing the selling price of electricity to the grid. As demonstrated in the 
sensitivity analysis, this model could also be extended to the optimal 
capacity configuration of different regions and scenarios through the 
modification of relevant input parameters. Furthermore, after refining 
the fundamental model and constraints, the methodology proposed in 
this study for simultaneous capacity configuration and scheduling 
optimization could also be employed for the optimal design of hybrid 
renewable energy systems with various combinations of energy 
equipment. 
6. Conclusions 
A novel simultaneous capacity configuration and scheduling opti -
mization approach is proposed for the PV/BESS integrated charging 
station in this study. In this approach, a hybrid modeling method for PV 
systems and an optimal scheduling method for charging piles are 
developed to accurately describe the source/load characteristics during 
the scheduling process. Besides, to effectively solve this model, the Big- 
M method is employed to reformulate this model into a MILP problem. 
The effectiveness of the reformulated model is validated based on a 
typical scenario of a commercial region in China. The results indicate 
that the proposed model could improve NPV by 15.67 % and reduce 
carbon emissions by 37.14 % compared to the entire grid-based charging 
station. Besides, integrating the PV hybrid modeling method and 
optimal charging scheduling strategy into the optimization model could 
achieve a superior capacity configuration and greatly improve the 
comprehensive performance of the system. Moreover, sensitivity anal -
ysis is implemented to analyze the impact of key parameters on the 
optimization results. The performances of the proposed method on 
different conditions are further analyzed, and some design guidelines for 
varying conditions are summarized. The proposed approach could pro -
vide a promising solution for the optimal capacity configuration and 
scheduling of PV/BESS integrated EV charging stations. However, the 
proposed model has not considered the accurate modeling of key in -
ternal state parameters for BESS and the impact of uncertainties in both 
internal and external parameters. In future work, the robust design and 
scheduling optimization methods considering accurate modeling of 
BESS and multi-parameter uncertainty would be further studied to 
achieve the optimal design of PV/BESS integrated charging stations.
